description: Simplify a document by cutting duplication and linking instead of paraphrasing
---

Simplify a document. If $ARGUMENTS names a file, simplify that file; otherwise simplify the document most recently discussed or edited in this session.

Read the whole document first, then revise it in place. Apply these rules:

1. Drop duplicated information. State each fact exactly once. If a detail appears in two sections (e.g. in prose and again in a list or acceptance criteria), keep it in the single most appropriate place and remove the others.
1. If information is already described elsewhere (another doc, a contract, a linked spec, an upstream source), link to it instead of paraphrasing it. Replace restated content with a concise reference to the canonical location.
1. Cut wording that restates context the reader already has, hedging, and filler. Prefer fewer words.
1. Preserve all substantive decisions, requirements, and intent. Simplifying means removing redundancy and indirection, not dropping meaning. If you are unsure whether something is redundant, keep it and ask.
1. Preserve the document's existing structure, formatting conventions, and voice. Do not introduce new emphasis, headings, or reformatting that wasn't requested.

After editing, briefly report what you cut and what you replaced with links.
